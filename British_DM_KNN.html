Double Modals in the British Isles

This image shows administrative regions in the British Isles with statistically significant values for the local autocorrelation statistic Getis-Ord Gi*, calculated using a binary spatial weights matrix and different numbers of nearest neighbors (from 4 to 30). The variable is the relative frequency of double modals per million words in transcripts from the CoBISE corpus.

Green hues indicate a statistically significant likelihood to have more double modals than the average for the entire British Isles, reddish hues fewer.
